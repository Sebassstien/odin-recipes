Pastelillo de Carne

Description

A Pastelillo de Carne is a classic Puerto Rican dish that resembles a savory turnover. It's made from a crispy and flaky pastry dough,
filled with different things. Once filled, the pastry is folded, sealed with a fork, and then deep-friend to a golden perfection. They are
a beloved street food and comfort food in Puerto Rico. They are commonly sold at food stands, festivals, and local bakeries.

Ready In: 1 hour, 10 minutes

Serves: 12

Ingredients: 10

  • 1 pound of lean ground beef
  • 1/3 cup of homemade sofrito
  • 1/3 cup of tomato sauce
  • 1 packet of sazon con achiote
  • 1 tablespoon of adobo all purpose seasoning
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 2 1/2 cups of small diced potatoes (frozen potatoes work too)
  • 1 package of Goya Brand- Empanada Discos con Achiote

Directions

  1. Defrost frozen empanada dough for 2-3 hours.
  2. On medium heat, cook homemade sofrito with tomato sauce until fragrant. Then add lean ground beef.
  3. Crumble the meat and season with sazon, adobo, cumin, dried oregano, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Once meat is brown, add diced potatoes and allow the remaining liquid to steam potatoes until tender. Cool your mixture.
  5. On a flat surface, sprinkle some all-purpose flour and lay out your empanada dough.
  6. Add a tablespoon of the meat mixture in the center of the dough. Fold over the dough and firmly press with your thumbs. To properly seal the seam, crimp the edges with a fork.
  7. In hot oil, fry the empanadas for 1 minute on each side. Let them rest on a paper towel and enjoy!
